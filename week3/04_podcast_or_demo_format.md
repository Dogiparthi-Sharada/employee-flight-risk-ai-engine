# 🎙️ Friday Recording — Walk-Through / Mini-Podcast Format

> Goal: a 15–25 minute conversational walk-through of AttriSense
> with a friend/peer/classmate. Posted as a "watch the deep-dive"
> piece of content for the audience that wants more depth.

---

## 🎯 Why do this

A 15-minute video shows things a post cannot:

- **Personality** — recruiters hire humans, not resumes
- **Communication ability** — *the* most under-tested skill in interviews
- **Real-time defense** — answering an unscripted question is the actual interview signal
- **Iteration** — proves you can talk about your work fluidly, not just write about it

It also doubles as **interview practice**. You'll get faster, calmer, and more concise every time you run through it.

## 👥 Who to invite

Best partners (ranked):

1. **A classmate from MSBA** who's also job-searching — peer-to-peer dynamic
2. **A friend in tech** who can ask probing questions but isn't an expert
3. **A non-technical friend** who can play the "explain it like I'm a hiring manager" role
4. **Yourself, solo** — if no one's available, a screen-record monologue works

What matters: **someone who will ask questions you don't fully expect.** That's the interview practice.

## 📋 Suggested 20-minute outline

| Time | Topic | What happens |
|---|---|---|

| 17:00–20:00 | What's next | Causal uplift, survival analysis, the patent angle. Wrap. |

## 🎬 Setup (15 min before recording)

- [ ] Camera at eye level
- [ ] Soft front lighting (window or lamp behind the camera, not behind you)
- [ ] Plain background or clean blurred background
- [ ] Headphones / Lavalier mic if available
- [ ] Streamlit dashboard running locally on a *different* tab/screen
- [ ] Architecture diagram open in another tab
- [ ] Glass of water within reach
- [ ] Phone on silent
- [ ] Test recording — 30 seconds, play back, check audio + video
- [ ] Snacks for after — recording is exhausting

---

## 🗣️ Speaking guidelines

- **Pace:** Slower than you think you need to. 130-150 words per minute.
- **Pauses:** Are fine. They edit out beautifully.
- **"Um/uh":** Don't worry about them on the first take — Descript or CapCut auto-removes them.
- **Mistakes:** Just keep going. You can edit later. Stopping mid-sentence ruins flow more than the original mistake.
- **Eye contact:** Look at the camera lens, not the screen, when answering.

---

## ✂️ Editing in CapCut (post-recording, ~1 hour)

1. Trim the first 30 seconds and last 30 seconds (always too rough)
2. Cut any moment >5 seconds of silence
3. Add captions (auto-generate, then proofread)
4. Add a title card: *"AttriSense Walk-Through · Sharada Dogiparthi · 2026"*
5. Add an end card: GitHub URL + LinkedIn URL
6. Soft background music at 5% volume (optional)
7. Export at 1080p (16:9 for YouTube, 1:1 for LinkedIn)

## 🎯 Why this is interview gold

Hiring managers will absolutely watch a 20-minute walk-through if you send it to them. Most candidates don't make one. The ones who do convert at 3-4× the average rate because:

1. They've already seen you communicate clearly
2. They've already seen you defend your work
3. The interview becomes a *follow-up*, not a *first encounter*
4. They can share the link internally to advocate for you

Send the YouTube link in any post-interview thank-you email:

> *"P.S. — recorded a fuller walk-through last week if useful for your team: [link]. Happy to do a similar one tailored to a Lumentum context if there's interest."*
