# 🔁 Re-Engagement Templates — 2nd Touch on Cold Leads

> By Week 3, some Week 2 DMs / applications haven't replied.
> Rule: send ONE polite second touch. No more.
> If the second touch doesn't reply, leave them alone for 90 days.

---

## 🎯 Universal rules for 2nd touch

1. **Reference the original message.** Don't pretend it's the first time.
2. **Add new information.** A new artifact (paper accepted, post that landed, demo shipped) gives them a reason to engage.
3. **Lower the ask.** If you asked for a call, downgrade to "any feedback is useful."
4. **End with full grace.** "Completely understand if timing isn't right" — removes pressure, increases reply rate.
5. **Send Tuesday-Thursday morning.** Avoid Mondays (overflow inbox) and Fridays (weekend brain).

## 🚨 The 90-day rule

After the 2nd touch, if there's no reply:

- **Wait 90 days minimum.**
- During those 90 days, **do NOT** like, comment on, or otherwise visibly engage with their LinkedIn (it looks needy).
- After 90 days, you can reach out again **only if you have a substantively new artifact** — a new paper, a new role start, a major version of the project.

Repeated cold outreach to the same person without new value erodes your credibility.

---

## 📊 Reply-rate benchmarks

Realistic data from cold outreach campaigns:

| Touch | Reply rate (B2B/professional) |
|---|---|
| 1st cold message | 8-15% |
| 2nd touch (well-written) | 12-20% |
| 3rd touch | <5% (and looks desperate) |

This means: out of 50 first-touch DMs, expect ~5-7 replies. Of those, ~2-3 lead to real conversations. Of those, ~1 leads to a job-relevant outcome.

That's normal. **Volume matters.** 50 DMs over 4 weeks is realistic and high-leverage.

---

## 💡 Pro tip — the "P.S." line

Adding a P.S. with a specific, low-pressure offer increases reply rate by ~30%:

> *P.S. — if a 15-minute conversation isn't the right shape, even a quick reply with one sentence about [specific thing] would be incredibly useful.*

It gives them an easy way to half-engage instead of full-decline.
