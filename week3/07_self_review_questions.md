## 🚦 Section 4 — The Strategic Pivot Check (5 min)

Answer yes / no:

- [ ] Are the **right companies** engaging? (If you wanted Lumentum and got 5 random startups, the targeting needs work.)
- [ ] Are the **right roles** matching the inbound? (If you want AI/ML and recruiters DM about generic data analyst, your headline isn't doing its job.)
- [ ] Is the **content compounding**? (Each post should drive new followers and new DMs, not the same audience reacting to everything.)
- [ ] Is your **energy holding**? (Three weeks in, burnout starts to lurk. Be honest.)

If 3+ are "no", **Week 4 is a pivot week**, not a continuation.

---

## 🔄 Section 5 — Possible Week 4 Pivots

If something needs to change:

### Pivot 1 — **Niche down further**
- Stop generic AI/ML tags
- Start hyper-targeting *People Analytics* specifically
- Find 5 specific People Analytics leaders, engage with their posts daily

### Pivot 2 — **Switch from posting to direct outreach**
- Posting got you visibility. Time to convert.
- Pause public posts for the week
- Send 50 deeply researched, personalized DMs

### Pivot 3 — **Live demo events**
- Host a 30-min live demo on LinkedIn Live or X Spaces
- Single live event > 3 written posts for trust building
- Invite specific people via DM

### Pivot 4 — **Different platform**
- If LinkedIn isn't yielding, try:
  - Reddit r/datascience or r/MachineLearning
  - Hacker News (Show HN)
  - Discord/Slack communities (MLOps, Data Engineering, People Analytics)

### Pivot 5 — **Pause and rest**
- 7 days completely offline
- Then come back with v2 of the project (causal uplift, etc.)
- Re-launch with a fresh story

**ALL of these are valid.** The wrong move is to keep doing what's not working out of momentum/sunk-cost.

## 🎁 Section 6 — The Only Question That Matters (5 min)

> *will I have an offer in hand by June 14?"*

Don't overthink it. The honest answer is the right answer.

## 💌 Section 7 — A Note to Yourself

Reserve the last 5 minutes for yourself, not the campaign.

Job searches are emotionally brutal. The same content gets celebrated one week and ignored the next. The same conversation goes great and converts to silence. None of it is personal, but all of it feels personal.

Three things worth remembering:

1. **You shipped.** Most students don't. The fact that there is a project, a paper, a demo, and a campaign at all puts you ahead of 95% of the field.
2. **The job will come.** It might be Lumentum. It might be the third company. It might be one you haven't heard of yet. The trajectory is good. Trust it.
3. **Be kind to yourself this week.** Eat real food. Walk outside. Call a friend who has nothing to do with the job search. The campaign will wait.

You're doing it right.

Onward.
