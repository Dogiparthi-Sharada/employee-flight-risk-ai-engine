## 🎯 Why this post matters

Three things happen when you publish a "month in review" post at the end of a launch:

1. **Narrative consolidation** — anyone who saw any single post in the past month gets the full arc. Repeat visibility, low cost.
2. **Loop closure** — you signal that you finish what you start. Recruiters value this disproportionately.
3. **Compounding follow** — followers who joined late can scroll *backward*. Each prior post gets a fresh wave of reactions for free.

The "month in review" is also one of the highest-converting posts for inbound interest because it shows velocity in a single screenshot.

## 🎯 Tagging strategy

LinkedIn allows ~10 tags per post. Use them:

- **3-5 specific people** who genuinely helped — faculty, classmates, peers who reviewed work, recruiters who took calls. **Tag only with their permission** or only those who'd clearly want to be tagged.
- **2-3 organizations:** California State University East Bay, Lumentum (only if interview went somewhere), specific community groups
- **Do NOT** tag random influencers or hiring managers without prior interaction. Looks needy.

## 📊 What "good" looks like

This post tends to outperform launch posts because the audience now exists:

| Metric | Below | Average | Strong |
|---|---|---|---|
| Impressions | <3,000 | 8,000 | 25,000+ |
| Reactions | <80 | 250 | 800 |
| Comments | <15 | 50 | 150 |
| New followers | <30 | 80 | 250 |
| Inbound DMs (in 72 hr) | <8 | 25 | 80 |

The **inbound DM volume** is the metric that matters most for the job search. A "month in review" post is the highest single-shot inbound generator on LinkedIn.

---

## 🎯 What NOT to include

- ❌ A list of every job you applied for (looks desperate)
- ❌ Salary specifics (almost never appropriate on LinkedIn)
- ❌ Negative talk about other candidates / approaches / companies
- ❌ Promises of what you'll do "next" without a date
- ❌ "I'm proud of myself" language (the work is proud — let the reader feel it)

The voice is **calm, grateful, specific, forward-looking.** Not triumphant.
