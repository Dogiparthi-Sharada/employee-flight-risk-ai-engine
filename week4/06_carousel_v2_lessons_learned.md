## 🎴 6-slide structure

### Slide 1 — Hook
```
5 things I learned shipping
AttriSense in public
(month-long retrospective)
```
Visual: simple title card, your headshot or repo logo, MSBA / CSUEB credit.

### Slide 2 — Lesson 1: Posting was harder than coding.
```
LESSON 1
Posting was harder than coding.

I had the model, the RAG, and the dashboard before I had a single
public LinkedIn post.

The technical work has clear stopping rules. Posting requires
deciding the work is good enough — and that's a different muscle.

Practice it before you need it.
```

### Slide 3 — Lesson 2: The boring posts converted best.
```
LESSON 2
The boring posts converted best.

The viral hooks brought impressions.
The technical deep-dives brought conversations.
The "3 things I got wrong" brought offers.

Audience size is vanity. Audience quality is leverage.
```

### Slide 4 — Lesson 3: Specificity beats polish.
```
LESSON 3
Specificity beats polish.

"Random Forest with SMOTE in the training fold only" beat
"a robust ML pipeline."

"PR-AUC 0.74 vs 0.41 baseline" beat "high-performing model."

If you're hiding behind vague language, the work isn't ready.
If the work is ready, name it.
```

### Slide 5 — Lesson 4: AI tooling doesn't shrink the bar — it raises it.
```
LESSON 4
AI tooling doesn't shrink the bar — it raises it.

I built faster because of Copilot, Claude, and ChatGPT. So did
every other candidate I'm competing with.

What still differentiates: which problem to pick, which design
to defend, which trade-off to accept, which output to throw away.

Generation is cheap. Judgment is the moat.
```

### Slide 6 — Lesson 5: Done > perfect. Always.
```
LESSON 5
Done > perfect. Always.

I shipped a v1 with bugs.
I posted before the demo was deployed.
I submitted the paper before I felt the title was right.

Every imperfection got better in v2 because v1 was real.
Nothing got better while I was waiting.

The cost of waiting is invisible.
The cost of shipping is just embarrassment — and embarrassment
is survivable.

Ship anyway.

— Sharada Dogiparthi
  github.com/Dogiparthi-Sharada/attrisense
```

## 📊 Why this carousel works

- ✅ Saves all the audience effort of finding signal in your prior posts
- ✅ Carousels keep impressions trickling for 5-7 days vs single-post 24 hours
- ✅ The closing line ("Ship anyway") is screenshot-able and gets re-shared
- ✅ Lesson 4 (AI tooling) directly addresses the resume-skepticism problem in a way that strengthens, not weakens, your candidacy
- ✅ Counter-positions you against the avalanche of "10x your output with AI" posts — you're the calm voice that distinguishes generation from judgment
