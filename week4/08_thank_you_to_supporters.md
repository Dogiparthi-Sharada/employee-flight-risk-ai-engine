# 💌 Personal Thank-You Notes — Templates

> Send these one-by-one over Week 4. Not LinkedIn posts. Not group emails.
> Real, specific, named messages to the people who carried you here.

> **Why this matters:** the people who helped you in months 1-4 are the people you will help in your career for the next 30 years. The single highest-ROI hour of your week is writing 5 personal notes.

---

## ✉️ Template T1 — Faculty mentor / advisor

Send via email. Or, even better, handwrite and mail.

```
Dear Dr. [Last Name],

I wanted to send a short note to thank you properly — separate
from any class email or formal submission.

When I started AttriSense, I had the technical pieces but no
real sense of what would make the work worth defending. The
[specific thing they did — feedback session / paper review / 
office-hours conversation about Section IV] reshaped how I
framed the imbalanced-classification piece. It also reshaped
how I think about presenting work for the rest of my career.

A quick update on where it's gone:
• Open-sourced under MIT — github.com/Dogiparthi-Sharada/attrisense
• Preprint posted on arXiv
• [Submitted to / accepted at / under review at] IEEE Big Data
  Industry Track
• [Patent Pending via CSUEB Tech Transfer / USPTO Provisional]
• Currently interviewing across the Bay Area; [if relevant: with
  Lumentum specifically]

I'd love to keep you in the loop as it evolves. And — once
you've recovered from the semester — I'd value the chance to
buy you coffee and hear what you'd push me to do next.

Sincerely,
Sharada Dogiparthi
MSBA, CSUEB
[email]
```

**Why this works:**
- Specific (names what they actually did)
- Updates them on outcomes (not asking for more)
- Asks for time politely, conditional on their schedule
- Signals you'll keep them in the loop — most students don't, and faculty notice

## ✉️ Template T3 — Classmate / peer reviewer

LinkedIn DM is fine.

```
Hey [Name],

Wanted to send a non-public thank-you. Your feedback on
[specific piece — Slide 4 of the carousel / the SHAP code review /
the early read of the paper] genuinely changed the work. The
[specific outcome — caught the SMOTE leak / cleaned up the
storytelling / reframed Section II] was the single highest-impact
revision in the whole month.

I owe you. If there's ever a project, paper, or job thing where
I can return the favor — please tell me. Means it.

Sharada
```

**Why this works:**
- *"Genuinely changed the work"* is specific and rare praise — peers remember it
- *"I owe you"* sets up future reciprocity, which is the real long-term currency

## ✉️ Template T5 — Recruiter or hiring manager who took your call

```
Hi [Name],

Whether or not the [Role] process at [Company] converts to an
offer, I wanted to send a personal thank-you separately from the
formal process.

You [specific thing — gave 90 minutes when you owed me 30 / pushed
me hard on Section IV during the technical screen / sent the
follow-up email even when there was no update]. Few candidates
get treated that way; I notice when I do.

If our paths cross professionally again — at [Company] or
elsewhere — I would value that.

Sharada Dogiparthi
[email]
```

**Why this works:**
- Sent **regardless of outcome** — distinguishes you from the 95% who only thank-you when they get the offer
- Specific praise ("90 minutes when you owed me 30") shows you noticed
- Closes politely without asking for anything

This single template — sent post-rejection — has reactivated more pipelines for more candidates than any other follow-up approach. Recruiters remember. They circle back when the next role opens.

## ✉️ Template T6 — Family / partner

Don't write this in template form. Write it by hand.

But the components are:

```
Acknowledge the cost they bore (weekends, dinners, your bandwidth).
Name a specific moment they showed up.
Say what their support actually meant — not abstractly.
Promise nothing. Just thank them.
```

The job search is a household project, not a personal one. Recognizing that on paper matters more than recognizing it in passing.

- These are the highest-leverage relationships of the next 5 years
- Costs you 5 minutes; converts the stranger into a permanent allies

## 💎 The single rule across all of these

**Be specific.**

Not: *"Thank you for everything."*
But: *"The 12-minute conversation on March 4 about the SMOTE-leak issue saved me from publishing a flawed paper."*

Not: *"Your support meant a lot."*
But: *"You rearranged a Tuesday meeting so I could take the Lumentum interview. I noticed."*

Specificity is the entire mechanism by which these notes work. Generic gratitude is forgettable. Specific gratitude is unforgettable — and the people who receive it become the spine of your professional network for the next 30 years.

## 🚦 Suggested cadence

Don't try to send all 7 in one day. Spread across Week 4:

| Day | Send to |
|---|---|
| Mon | Faculty mentor (T1) |
| Tue | Family / partner (T6) — handwritten |
| Wed | Top 2 classmate / peer (T3) |
| Thu | Top 1 mentor / coach (T4) |
| Fri | Tech Transfer (T2) — only if relevant |
| Sat | Recruiter / HM (T5) — every active and recently-closed process |
| Sun | Stranger DMs (T7) — pick top 2-3 |

Total: ~10-15 personal notes. ~3 hours of work across the week.

Highest-ROI work in the entire campaign.
