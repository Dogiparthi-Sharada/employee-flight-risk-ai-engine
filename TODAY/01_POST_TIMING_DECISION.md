## Why posting before the interview is a trap

### 1. Recruiters / hiring managers check LinkedIn pre-interview

Standard practice: most hiring managers refresh your LinkedIn 15-60 minutes before the call. If a 2-hour-old "Look at my AttriSense project!" post is the top thing they see, three things happen — none good:

- It reads as **timed for them** (because it was). Feels manipulative.
- It anchors the conversation around the post instead of you.
- It makes you look **transactional**, not generous.

### 2. Asymmetric risk

| Outcome of interview | If you posted before | If you didn't post before |
|---|---|---|
| ⭐ Goes great | Post lands ~80% as well as it would have post-interview | Post lands 10× better next week — momentum compounds |
| 😐 Goes okay | Post is fine | Post is fine |
| 💔 Goes badly | Post is forever associated with the rejection in your head | Clean slate. Post next week with a fresh story. |

The downside of NOT posting today is zero. The downside of posting today and the interview going sideways is permanent.

### 3. The interview itself IS the demo

The most powerful version of this work is **walking the hiring manager through it live**, not pointing them at a public post. You get:
- Real-time face reactions
- Their actual questions, not LinkedIn's
- Permission to defend it under pressure (which proves you can)

That's not just better than a post — it's *the* hiring signal a post can never replicate.

### 4. The 4-week campaign is engineered for Tuesday May 12

The whole launch sequence — week2/01_launch_day_playbook.md, the Twitter thread, the cold DMs, the carousel pre-roll — assumes Tuesday morning publication. Posting today breaks the calibration.
