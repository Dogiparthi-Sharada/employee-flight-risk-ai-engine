# 🔐 How to Share the Repo Without Triggering Corporate IT Issues

> Concern: sharing a project link/file from your current job environment
> may trigger your employer's DLP, monitoring, or "moonlighting" policies.
> Concern: the *interviewing* company's IT may block GitHub, big files, etc.
>
> Solution: pick the lowest-friction channel. They're ranked below.

---

## 🥇 Best: spoken URL + public GitHub

**This is the answer. Almost always.**

The repo is **already public**. Sharing the URL is not "sharing" — it's pointing. There is no transmission, no attachment, no upload, no download trace inside any company's network unless someone clicks through.

```
https://github.com/Dogiparthi-Sharada/employee-flight-risk-ai-engine
```

(Or after rename: `https://github.com/Dogiparthi-Sharada/attrisense`)

**Say it aloud during the interview.** Have it on the title slide if you're presenting. Memorize it.

✅ Zero risk to your current employer
✅ Zero risk to the interviewer's employer
✅ Zero attachments anywhere
✅ Lets them explore on their schedule, not yours

---

## 🥈 Second-best: live demo URL

```
https://attrisense.streamlit.app    (or whatever your Streamlit URL is)
```

Same logic — public link, no transmission. Even better because the *interviewer doesn't have to clone or run anything.* They click and see.

**Best for:** the moment in the interview when someone says "can you show me?"

---

## 🥉 Third: QR code on resume / portfolio PDF

Generate one free at [qr-code-generator.com](https://www.qr-code-generator.com).

Put it on:
- The bottom of your resume (1-page version)
- The "Projects" section of any leave-behind PDF
- The first slide of any presentation deck

**Why:** the interviewer scans on their *personal phone*, not their corporate device. Zero corp-network footprint. Zero awkwardness.

## 5️⃣ LinkedIn DM with the link

Fine, but second-tier. LinkedIn DMs leave a more visible trail than email and some people don't check them.

If they explicitly prefer LinkedIn — fine. Otherwise, default to personal email.

---

## 🚫 What to AVOID

| ❌ Method | Why it's bad |
|---|---|
| Sending a ZIP of source code | Triggers DLP / antivirus / "what is this large attachment" — looks suspicious even if innocent |
| Google Drive / Dropbox link | Some company firewalls block these on principle |
| Sending from your CURRENT employer's email | "Moonlighting" red flag for your employer; "are you about to leave" red flag for the interviewer |
| Sending from your CURRENT employer's laptop | Some corp networks log all outbound traffic. Use phone hotspot. |
| Using your CURRENT company's Slack/Teams to coordinate | Almost always logged. Risk to your employment. |
| Mentioning the project AT WORK to anyone | Some employers' IP-assignment clauses can claim work done while employed there. **AttriSense is your MSBA capstone. Period. Talk about it as such.** |

## ⚠️ Specific scenarios

### "The interviewer says GitHub is blocked on their network."

```
"Totally understand. Live demo is at attrisense.streamlit.app —
 should work over standard HTTPS. If that's also blocked, I'm
 happy to walk you through it on my screen-share right now."
```

Now you've turned a blocker into a screen-share — which is BETTER.

### "Their HR wants the source code to verify."

This is unusual but legitimate. Two responses:

> *"Repo is public on GitHub — you can clone it directly. Happy to send a tarball to a personal email if that's easier; I'd ask not to send to a corporate address from a personal one for IT-policy reasons on my end."*

Then send a tarball / ZIP from personal email to whatever address they specify, **once**, with a polite note.

### "Your interviewer asks during the call to 'send everything right now.'"

Don't, mid-call. Polite deflect:

> *"Let me put together a clean summary email tonight — that way you'll have one place with all the links and the right context."*

Then send the email at 7 PM the same day with the polished thank-you note.

### "Your current employer might find out you're interviewing."

This is a real concern. Three rules:

1. **Don't take the interview from a corporate conference room or laptop.** Use a personal device + your home Wi-Fi or phone hotspot.
2. **Don't post about AttriSense from your work network or hours.** All public-repo activity should happen on personal time + personal device.
3. **If asked at work why you're "out" today** — keep it vague: "personal appointment." You owe nobody more than that.

---

## 💎 The principle

Sharing a **link** to public material is fundamentally different from sharing a **file**. Always default to the link.

Public GitHub + public Streamlit demo + public arXiv preprint = a portfolio that no firewall scans, no DLP system flags, no employer can claim, and no interviewer needs to download anything to see.

**The best portfolio is the one that requires zero permission to view.**

You already built it. Just point at it.
