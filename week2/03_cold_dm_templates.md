# 📨 Cold-But-Warm DM Templates

> "Cold-but-warm" = stranger, but specific enough to feel personal.
> Aim for ~80% reply rate by being *specific* and *short*.
> Send 5–10 per day during Week 2. Track in `08_pipeline_tracker.md`.

---

## 🎯 Universal rules

1. **Open with a specific reference** — to their post, their company's news, their paper, their GitHub repo. Generic openers ("Hi, I'd love to connect!") get auto-deleted.
2. **State your ask in one sentence.** Most people skim DMs in 5 seconds.
3. **Make it easy to say yes.** Offer a 15-minute call, not "would love to chat sometime."
4. **No links in the first message.** LinkedIn flags DMs with links as spam. Drop the link in the *reply* after they respond.
5. **One paragraph max.** ~60 words. Mobile-readable.
6. **Sign with full name + program** — proves you're real, not a bot.

## 🚨 What NOT to do in DMs

- ❌ Don't open with "Hi" and nothing else (waiting for them to reply before you send the real message)
- ❌ Don't send 10 emoji
- ❌ Don't send a wall of text
- ❌ Don't send the same DM to 50 people in 1 day (LinkedIn flags as spam — your account can be limited)
- ❌ Don't ask for a referral on the first DM
- ❌ Don't say "circling back" — say what you're actually following up about
- ❌ Don't @mention them in a public post hoping they'll reply — that's noise, not signal
