## 📌 Tips for sending this email

- **Send Tuesday or Wednesday morning, 9–11 AM** — best response rate from faculty
- **CC nobody.** Single-recipient emails get faster responses
- **Subject line keywords matter** — "co-author" + "IEEE paper" makes them open it
- **Attach PDF, not LaTeX** — most faculty want to read in 5 minutes, not compile
- **If they say no:** still ask for a 30-min meeting for feedback. Faculty rarely refuse advice; co-authorship is a higher bar

## 🔄 Variation if no specific advisor exists yet

Replace the second paragraph with:

> *"Although I haven't taken a class with you directly, your work on [their research area] aligns closely with what I've been building, and I'd value your perspective even if co-authorship isn't the right fit at this stage. Would you be open to a brief 30-minute conversation about the project?"*

Most faculty respond positively to specific, concrete asks from students who have shipped something real.
