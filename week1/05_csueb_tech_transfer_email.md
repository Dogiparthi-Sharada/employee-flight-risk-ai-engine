## 📌 What to expect after sending

1. **Response within 5–10 business days** — university tech-transfer offices are not Silicon Valley fast.
2. **They'll send the IDF form** — a 2–4 page invention-disclosure document. Fill it out *carefully and conservatively* — only claim novelty for things genuinely novel.
3. **30-minute consultation** — likely with a tech-transfer officer, possibly with university legal counsel. They will ask about prior art, public disclosure status, and your funding sources during development.
4. **Decision** — within 30–60 days the office will either:
   - **(a)** Elect to file a provisional patent (cost covered by university; you keep co-inventor status; revenue split per CSUEB IP policy)
   - **(b)** Pass on filing → you keep all rights and can file independently
5. **If (a):** add *"🪪 Patent Pending"* to the README and your LinkedIn headline. Don't disclose claim details publicly until the provisional is filed.
6. **If (b):** your $130 USPTO micro-entity provisional is still cheap. Or use defensive publication via the arXiv preprint.
