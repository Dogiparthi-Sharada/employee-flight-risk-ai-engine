# 📧 Post-Interview Thank-You Email

> Send within **60 minutes** of the interview ending. Today.
> Yes, even if the interview went badly. Especially if it went badly.

---

## 🎯 Version B — Felt rocky, want a recovery shot

Same as above, but add this paragraph after the project links:

> *"Reflecting on our conversation, I realized I could have given a tighter answer when you asked about [X]. The fuller answer is [Y]. I'd welcome the chance to walk through the project in more depth if useful — I think a screen-share would let me show some of the design trade-offs that are easier to demonstrate than describe."*

This is **gold.** Most candidates panic-vanish after a rocky interview. A self-aware recovery email can turn a "no" into a "let's do another round."

---

## 🎯 Version C — Want to pivot toward a different role

Same as Version A, but replace the closing paragraph with:

> *"In addition to the [original role], if you're aware of openings on [adjacent team — data engineering, ML platform, business analytics] where the AttriSense work might be a fit, I'd appreciate any introductions. I want to be deliberate about finding the right team, not just the closest open headcount."*

Recruiters appreciate candidates who help them route — and many internships get filled through cross-team referrals.

---

## ✅ Rules for thank-you emails

1. **Send within 60 minutes** — 24h is the ceiling, not the target
2. **One specific reference** to the conversation (proves you were listening)
3. **Keep it short** — under 200 words. Nobody reads long emails.
4. **No re-asking the same questions** — the email is for thanks + addenda, not interrogation
5. **Don't ask "when will I hear back?"** — it sounds anxious. They will tell you when they're ready.
6. **One clean signature block** — name, program, phone, LinkedIn. Nothing else.
7. **Send to the recruiter AND any technical interviewers** if you have their emails. Personalize each one with a different specific reference.

---

## 🚨 Don't do this

- ❌ "I really hope you'll consider me for this role" — sounds desperate
- ❌ "I noticed you went to [school]" — only if it came up naturally
- ❌ "Here are 5 more documents" — overkill, already sent in the interview
- ❌ Long apologies — one short paragraph max if you must address something
- ❌ Asking for feedback in the same email — wait for a "no" first, then ask
- ❌ Sending it from a personal email ending in `91princess` or `xX_GamerGirl_Xx@gmail.com` — use [first.last]@horizon.csueb.edu or a clean Gmail
