## 🎨 Editing tips (CapCut)

- **Music:** Soft tech-bed at 20% volume. CapCut's free royalty-free library has good options. Search "corporate inspirational" or "minimal tech."
- **Captions:** **Always burn them in.** 85% of LinkedIn views are sound-off. Use CapCut's auto-caption feature, then style: white text, black drop shadow, bottom-third positioning.
- **Cuts:** Every 4–6 seconds. Don't let any one shot last more than 6 seconds.
- **Zoom-ins:** Add a subtle 1.05× → 1.10× zoom on the NL→SQL typing moment and the employee drill-down. Makes it feel kinetic.
- **End card:** Last 3 seconds = static frame with GitHub URL + "AttriSense" + your name. Hold this clearly so people can screenshot.
- **Export:**
  - Resolution: **1080×1080** (square — best LinkedIn feed performance)
  - Frame rate: 30fps
  - Format: MP4 (H.264)
  - Max file size for LinkedIn native upload: **5GB**, but aim for under 100MB

## 🎤 If you're nervous about your voice

Three options, ranked:

1. **Record your own voice** — *strongly preferred*. Authenticity wins on LinkedIn. Use AirPods or a $30 Lavalier mic. Practice the script 3 times before recording.
2. **ElevenLabs voice clone** — Clone your voice in 10 minutes from a 1-minute sample, then have it read the script. Free tier is enough.
3. **No voiceover, just music + captions** — Perfectly acceptable. Use a slightly slower pacing (75 seconds total) and richer captions to compensate.
